Data, codes and preprints

I strongly advocate for an open and reproducible research model and culture that may ultimate contribute to a more accessible, transparent and reliable science. This led me to co-found Open Plant Pathology Initiative. In my Lab, we make use of R language and Tidyverse for all statistical and data science related activities. All data and computational codes produced during our research are made available ahead of peer-review. You can find our code on GitHub. The research outcomes are organized and shared as a research compendium and preprints of manuscripts are archived in Open Science Framework (OSF).
